A low-cost waste sorter that looks at what you're about to throw out and tells you which stream it belongs in. The point is to catch contamination at the bin, before the wrong item taints a whole batch downstream.

## Approach

An EfficientNet-B2 classifier fine-tuned on a mix of TACO, TrashNet, and CompostNet. A Flask service runs inference and talks to a small Next.js frontend (React, Chakra UI). The whole thing sits on a Raspberry Pi at the bin, so capture and classification happen locally with no round-trip to the cloud.

## What was actually hard

Lighting at a real campus bin is not the lighting in any dataset. Most of the engineering was getting the capture path and the model behaving under that drift, not chasing accuracy on a clean test set. We deployed on-site at Johns Hopkins to see how it held up against real users.
