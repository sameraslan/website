A dataset and benchmark for testing whether long-context language models actually remember earlier turns of a conversation, not just whether they can be cued to fetch a needle from a haystack.

## Why this benchmark

Most long-context evals measure retrieval: hide a sentence, ask for it back, score the recall. That tells you the context window works mechanically. It says nothing about whether a model will bring back something a human conversational partner would naturally bring back, a callback, a contradiction, an earlier preference, without being asked.

## What we measure

Multi-turn dialogues where the natural next thing for the model to say requires it to surface earlier content on its own. We score with held-out conversational patterns and compare out-of-the-box LLMs against memory-augmented setups, which lets us isolate where each one fails.
