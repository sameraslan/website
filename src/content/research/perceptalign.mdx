A model that generates audio for a silent video and lands the sound when the event happens on screen, not a few hundred milliseconds late. The hard part isn't acoustic plausibility, it's synchronization.

## Approach

A transformer that conditions on visual stream features and predicts spectrogram frames, trained with a Perceptual Alignment Loss (PAL) that penalizes timing drift independently from acoustic quality. Inference runs online with a short look-ahead window calibrated to the video frame rate.

## Why it matters

Most video-to-audio work optimizes offline plausibility. Real-time alignment is the constraint that actually makes the output usable when a human is watching: accessibility tools, prototyping, captioning sound for hearing-impaired viewers.
